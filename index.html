Overview

We propose SkillBlender, a framework for humanoid whole-body control, that first pretrains individual goal-conditioned primitive skills, and then dynamically blends them for complex loco-manipulation tasks. We also present SkillBench, a parallel, cross-embodiment, and diverse simulated benchmark containing 3 embodiments, 4 primitive skills, and 8 loco-manipulation tasks. Our simulation-trained primitive skills are also physically deployable in the real world.

Method Pipeline

Overview of SkillBlender. We first pretrain goal-conditioned primitive expert skills that are task-agnostic, reusable, and physically interpretable, and then reuse and blend these skills to achieve complex whole-body loco-manipulation tasks given only one or two task-specific reward terms.

Experiment Results

Qualitative Comparison

Choose task:



Qualitative comparison between different methods on three embodiments. Our SkillBlender not only achieves higher task success accuracy, but also avoids reward hacking and generates more feasible movements.

Skill Decomposition


Visualization of whole-body per-joint weights at different task stages. More blue means more Reaching, and more green means more Walking. This visualization highlights the spatial-temporal decomposition of our skill blending, where the two skills interleave rather than one skill dominating the overall motion.

Skill Deployment


Demonstrations of our primitive skill sim-to-real deployment. We control the primitive skills to perform various task-agnostic periodical movements. In future works, we aim to distill our state-based high-level task policies into vision-based policies and directly deploy them to the real world.
